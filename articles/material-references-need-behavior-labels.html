Material references need behavior labels, not just mood boards

Published 2026-06-28 - Updated 2026-06-28

visual-referencematerial-referencearchiveresearchworkflow

Open original source

A cluster of unpromoted visual-reference notes is useful because each source preserves a material behavior, not only an attractive image. For Knowledge Hub, these references should be filed by what a future project might need to retrieve: bamboo curvature, field-observation texture, ornament linework, atmospheric depth, or fluid deformation.

Architecture references should name structure and material

The Bamboo Craft Village source note is strongest as a study of curved bamboo structure. The useful retrieval reason is not only that the building looks organic. It is the way a cultural facility uses a looping roof form, local craft context, and bamboo material language to make structure and regional identity visible at the same time.

Nature notebooks preserve observation habits

The Jo Brown source note belongs beside visual-reference material because the notebook is a method, not only a book object. Mushroom shapes, watercolor texture, handwritten notes, and page sequencing preserve how close observation turns into a reusable drawing habit.

Ornament archives need linework metadata

The Petrie decorative-art note is a durable archive lead because the value is searchable linework and classification. A future design or research task may need geometric motifs, ancient ornament comparison, or a primary visual source rather than a modern mood-board crop. That makes the Project Gutenberg and PDF references worth promoting to Library.

Atmosphere and physics are both behavior references

The Hishida Shunso note and microgravity water note look unrelated at first, but both are retrieval problems around behavior. One is about mist, height, and spatial quiet in a painting; the other is about how water changes when surface tension dominates in microgravity. In both cases, the archive should preserve the behavior being studied before it preserves a broad aesthetic label.

What to preserve in Knowledge Hub

Future material-reference Wire entries should record the behavior, medium, source type, and likely reuse path: structural curve, surface texture, hand-drawn ornament, atmospheric depth, fluid deformation, or observational method. That makes the source useful for design, look development, drawing practice, and research without flattening every image into a generic visual-reference tag.
