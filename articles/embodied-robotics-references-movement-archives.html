A robotics source can be useful even before the hardware claim is fully evaluated. The durable layer for Knowledge Hub is the movement language: how a mechanism articulates, corrects balance, transfers weight, and recovers from constraint.

What the source preserves

The Rothheim joint post should stay lightweight in Wire, but it deserves promotion because it names a specific motion-reference problem: articulation under physical constraint. The useful details to retrieve later are not only the joint name. They are balance correction, constrained rotation, recovery movement, and weight-transfer timing.

Why this belongs near animation

Robotics motion references overlap with stop-motion blocking, creature rig behavior, procedural animation, embodied AI, and simulation-driven cinematics. A good movement archive lets a future shot or rigging problem ask: what kind of mechanical behavior was I trying to remember?

Production implication

The long-term archival value may be behavioral vocabulary rather than hardware specificity. Knowledge Hub should preserve this as a movement-reference item and revisit it when enough related robotics or physical-animation sources accumulate.
