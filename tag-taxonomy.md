# Knowledge Hub Tag Taxonomy

## needs-review

Entries that require later editorial classification, verification, or cleanup before being treated as stable knowledge.

## ai

General artificial-intelligence systems, models, tools, and production implications.

## ai-video

AI systems or references related to generated moving images, image-to-video, video-to-video, or cinematic motion synthesis.

## film

Film language, cinema history, production references, staging, or moving-image criticism.

## visual-reference

Images, shots, scenes, or design references saved for visual analysis and future creative use.

## animation

Animation craft, movement design, stop-motion, drawn animation, or animated-film reference.

## vfx

Visual-effects production, compositing, procedural workflows, simulation, rendering, or production pipeline material.

## compositing

Image compositing, color operations, keying, grading-adjacent workflows, or Nuke/Resolve-style VFX assembly.

## blender

Blender tools, add-ons, geometry nodes, rendering workflows, or 3D production practices inside Blender.

## houdini

Houdini, SideFX, procedural node networks, HIP examples, or technical workflow references.

## procedural

Procedural creation systems, node-based generation, reusable technical setups, or rule-driven production workflows.

## pipeline

Production infrastructure, repeatable asset flow, studio process design, or cross-tool production systems.

## workflow

Repeatable working methods, tool chains, setup patterns, or practical process notes.

## rendering

Rendering engines, materials, look development, real-time preview, lighting, or output-quality workflows.

## 3d

Three-dimensional graphics, modeling, scene construction, spatial assets, or general 3D production material.

## spatial-capture

3D capture, Gaussian splatting, photogrammetry, reconstruction, scanning, or volumetric media.

## motion-design

Motion graphics, design animation, Cavalry-style systems, or animation used as graphic communication.

## cavalry

Cavalry software, examples, learning resources, or procedural motion-design workflows in Cavalry.

## design-tools

Design utilities, typography tools, browser-based design software, layout helpers, or visual-production utilities.

## local-ai

Local models, offline inference, self-hosted AI tools, or local-first AI production infrastructure.

## agents

Agent systems, AI automation, tool-using models, orchestration, or software agents used for production and research.

## audio

Voice, sound, transcription, speech models, audio restoration, or audio production workflows.

## document-ai

OCR, document understanding, layout extraction, archives, or AI-assisted reading of structured documents.

## education

Learning systems, pedagogy, curriculum design, study tools, or knowledge-map construction.

## visualization

Visual explanation, mathematical visualization, dimensional intuition, charts, or graphics used to make abstractions understandable.

## literature

Books, authors, literary history, literary criticism, writing practice, or source trails for literary research.

## criticism

Critical analysis, interpretation, review, intellectual framing, or argument-focused commentary.

## research

Saved material used as a research trail, citation lead, technical reference, or future investigation source.

## production

Practical production use, professional workflow implications, prototyping, staffing, or creative operations.

## materialx

MaterialX or related open material-description workflows for rendering and look development.

## comfyui

ComfyUI workflows, nodes, graphs, or AI-image/video generation pipelines based around ComfyUI.

## obsidian

Obsidian, markdown knowledge bases, personal knowledge management, or external memory workflows.

## 3dgs

3D Gaussian Splatting and adjacent point-based or radiance-field reconstruction workflows.

## nerf

NeRF and adjacent neural radiance field methods for view synthesis, reconstruction, and spatial capture.

## knowledge-map

External memory, graph-like organization, source mapping, and structured knowledge systems.

## ai-coding

AI-assisted coding, coding agents, repository automation, project memory, and development workflows.

## tutorial

Learning material, walkthroughs, or practical demonstrations that can teach a repeatable method.

## archive

Source preservation, library organization, public-domain material, and reusable reference collections.

## implementation

Practical build notes, system design details, or production-facing implementation references.

## recommendation

Curated picks, watch/read/use suggestions, or editorial selection notes.

## browser-tools

Browser-based tools for design, motion, editing, prototyping, or lightweight production work.

## computer-vision

Vision models, reconstruction, detection, tracking, and image understanding research or tooling.
