For Knowledge Hub, the durable lesson is that AI-adjacent funding and product-roadmap claims need to be archived separately: money, governance, user trust, and actual feature plans are different facts.

Funding is not the same as a feature roadmap

The saved Wire item points to 80 Level's report on Blender clarifying its AI policy and amending Anthropic's role from Development Fund patronage to a one-time donation. Preserve that as source context, not as a broad claim about Blender adopting or rejecting every possible AI workflow. The useful archive distinction is narrower: funding status, donor relationship, policy language, and product plans should not be collapsed into one headline.

Open tools need trust metadata

Blender is already a durable Library item, but this source deserves a separate policy reference because it documents how an open creative tool responds when community trust, corporate money, and AI expectations collide. A future note about AI in Blender should be able to retrieve the governance surface without rereading a whole news cycle.

Workflow notes should keep boundaries visible

The practical production question is not whether AI can be near Blender. It is where the boundary sits: core application features, external connectors, documentation, user-created add-ons, automation scripts, training data, and funding relationships each carry different review requirements. That makes this a workflow-governance note as much as a news item.

What to preserve in Knowledge Hub

Future AI-in-creative-tool Wire entries should record the official policy page when available, the secondary report that surfaced the issue, what changed, and which claims are source facts versus editorial interpretation. That keeps tool governance searchable beside normal production references.
