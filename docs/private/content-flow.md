# Internal Content Flow Policy

This document is an internal operating note. It defines the editorial relationship between Wire, Articles, and Library.

## Wire

Wire is the full Like export layer.

It preserves all liked/source posts as raw signals, references, fragments, and potential leads. Wire should be broad, permissive, and high-volume. It is not expected to be fully interpreted at the point of capture.

## Articles

Articles are the interpretation layer derived from Wire.

Articles organize, compare, integrate, and develop information discovered in Wire. They should turn repeated signals into coherent observations, arguments, trends, or production notes.

## Library

Library is the wiki-like summary layer created from Articles.

Library entries are reusable concepts, tools, workflows, references, and stable summaries that emerge from Article-level synthesis. Library should be cleaner and more durable than Wire or Articles.

## Tagging Principle

Many tags are encouraged when they represent distinct ideas. Duplicate tags are harmful and should be normalized through the tag taxonomy.

Canonical tag rules:

- Use lower-case slug style for canonical tags.
- Preserve useful specificity.
- Merge spelling, capitalization, singular/plural, and synonym variants.
- Do not display unknown as a tag.
- Use needs-review for entries that require later classification, verification, or editorial cleanup.

## Direction

Wire should maximize capture.
Articles should maximize synthesis.
Library should maximize reuse.
